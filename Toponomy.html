Tokyo, formerly known as Edo, is the capital city of Japan and the most populated city in the world with 37.468 million people living in it. The former city of Edo
spans for 13,452 square kilometers throughout the Tokyo Bay. It is situated in the Kantō region, on the central-eastern coast of Honshu, the largest island of the Japanese
archipelago.

The name Tokyo (東京都) means 'Eastern Capital', and it is formed by the Kanjis 東 (east), 京 (capital) and 都 (metropilis).

Edo was a well known fishing village, and gained important political standing during the Tokugawa shogunate. During the Meiji Restoration the imperial capital was moved from Kyoto
to Edo and renamed as Tokyo. The city was devastated in two occasions, first by the Great Kantō earthquake, and during the Second World War by the Allies, although that hasn't
stopped it from becoming one of the most important financial centres of the modern world.
